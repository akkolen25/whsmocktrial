Walpole High School Mock Trial


First organized in 1985, the Mock Trial Program's tournament places high school students in a simulated courtroom to assume the roles of lawyers and witnesses in a hypothetical case. Thousands of students at more than 130 schools across the commonwealth are participating. More than 100 lawyers across the state will serve as volunteer coaches and judges.

About the Activity

At the start of the season, a case packet is distributed which holds all of the information about the fake case. It holds affidavits, evidence, diagrams and all of the necessities. Then, each student picks/ gets assigned a role, which they prep for.

There are a variety of roles, so you can choose what's right for you! Both witnesses and lawyers are an integral part of the team. If you like asking tricky questions, you might want to be a lawyer; where as if you are good at answering them, a witness may be more suitable.
